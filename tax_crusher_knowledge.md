THE REAL PROBLEM (why "the bottom" loses)
=================================================================
MYTH: "low-income people get screwed into 40%."
FACT: federal income tax for a single mom with 2 kids earning <~$30k is usually $0
  — and she often gets a REFUND via credits. The real bites are:
  1. SELF-EMPLOYMENT TAX = 15.3% on NET self-employment income over $400. This hits
     OnlyFans/cleaning/gig/AI-influencer income HARD because no boss withholds it.
  2. UNCLAIMED CREDITS = free money left on the table (EITC, CTC).
  3. PAID PREPARERS = $200-$500 to file what VITA does FREE.
THE FIX: deduct legitimate expenses (lowers the 15.3%), claim every credit (refund),
  file FREE (VITA/IRS Free File). That's the whole app.

=================================================================
FILING THRESHOLDS (federal — verify current year)
=================================================================
- Employee (W-2) single under 65: ~$14,600 (2024 standard deduction) — below this,
  usually no federal return needed.
- Self-employed: MUST file if net SE income > $400, REGARDLESS of total. So an
  OnlyFans/cleaning mom with any profit files — but she likely OWES $0 income tax
  and GETS credits. Filing is how she collects the credits.
- Single parent with dependents: higher standard deduction; owes $0 income tax at
  low income and qualifies for refundable credits. The $17,500 the user mentioned is
  in the right ZONE — at that income with kids she owes ~$0 federal income tax and
  can get a sizeable refund. (Verify exact brackets yearly.)

=================================================================
REFUNDABLE CREDITS (THE "MONEY BACK" — the app's headline feature)
=================================================================
These pay even if you owe $0 income tax. This is the gold for low-income moms.
1. EARNED INCOME TAX CREDIT (EITC): for working low/moderate income. 2023 max:
   - 1 child ~$3,995 | 2 children ~$6,604 | 3+ children ~$7,430. Phases out as
     income rises (roughly fully gone by ~$50k-63k depending on kids). ONLYFANS/
     gig/cleaning INCOME COUNTS as earned income. Huge for this audience.
2. CHILD TAX CREDIT (CTC): $2,000/child. Up to $1,600 of it is REFUNDABLE (Additional
   CTC) for low earners — meaning it comes as cash even with $0 tax liability.
3. DEPENDENT CARE CREDIT: for childcare so she can work (camp, daycare). Percentage
   of up to $3,000 (1 child) / $6,000 (2+) expenses. Non-refundable but valuable.
4. PREMIUM TAX CREDIT: if she buys ACA marketplace insurance, the credit lowers
   premiums; can be claimed on return.
5. EDUCATION / OTHER: American Opportunity / Lifetime Learning (if she's in school);
   Saver's Credit for low-income retirement contributions (see below).
APP ACTION: interview the user, compute which credits apply, auto-fill the forms.

=================================================================
ABOVE-THE-LINE DEDUCTIONS (reduce taxable income directly)
=================================================================
These cut BOTH income tax and the SE tax base. File on Schedule 1.
1. HALF OF SELF-EMPLOYMENT TAX: deduct 50% of the SE tax paid. Automatic, big.
2. SELF-EMPLOYED HEALTH INSURANCE: 100% of premiums deducted (if no employer plan).
3. RETIREMENT (SEP IRA / Solo 401k): contributions cut taxable income. Even $100/mo
   helps and builds wealth. Also unlocks the Saver's Credit.
4. HSA: if she has a high-deductible plan, contributions are deductible + tax-free.

=================================================================
SCHEDULE C BUSINESS DEDUCTIONS (slash the 15.3% SE tax)
=================================================================
Net SE profit = revenue - expenses. Every legit expense lowers the 15.3% she pays.
For the AI-influencer / creator / cleaning / gig mom, THESE APPLY:
- MILEAGE: driving to shoots, to clients, to the post office. 67¢/mile (2024). LOG it.
- HOME OFFICE: % of rent/utils for the space she works (editing, admin, storage).
- SOFTWARE / SUBSCRIPTIONS: image-gen tools, editing apps, scheduling tools, the
  Fanvue/OnlyFans cut is NOT deductible but the TOOLS are. (NOVA's stack = deductible.)
- EQUIPMENT: phone used for biz, camera, ring light, laptop — depreciate or Section
  179. Phone/internet: deduct the BUSINESS-use %.
- SUPPLIES: cleaning supplies (if cleaning biz), props, wardrobe for shoots (if
  "ordinary & necessary" to the brand — be careful, document), makeup.
- BANK/PAYMENT FEES: PayPal/OnlyFans/Fanvue processing fees, business bank fees.
- EDUCATION: courses that improve her trade (marketing, editing).
- WIFI/PHONE %: prorate honestly.
- QBI / SECTION 199A: 20% deduction on qualified business income (subject to
  thresholds — for low income it's generally FULLY available). Cuts taxable income 20%.
APP ACTION: expense tracker + category matches + auto-Schedule C.

=================================================================
SELF-EMPLOYMENT TAX REALITY (the number to attack)
=================================================================
- SE tax = 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare) on NET SE profit >$400.
- It is NOT reduced by the standard deduction — only by lowering NET profit via
  deductions above. So $10k profit = $1,530 SE tax; deduct $4k expenses → $6k net →
  $918 SE tax. That's $612 kept. The app's job = find those $4k of expenses.
- Quarterly estimates: if she'll owe >$1k, pay quarterly to avoid penalties. App
  reminds + calculates.

=================================================================
FILE FREE — DON'T GET FLEECED
=================================================================
- IRS FREE FILE: free federal filing for AGI under ~$79k (varies). IRS.gov/freefile.
- VITA: IRS Volunteer Income Tax Assistance — FREE in-person filing for low/moderate
  income, disabled, limited-English. The app should ROUTE users to VITA.
- DIRECT FILE: IRS pilot in some states — file directly free. Expanding.
- MILITAX: if she's military/veteran family.
THE "we don't screw them" promise = the app points to FREE filing and shows the
  preparer-fee she's avoiding. That's the differentiator vs TurboTax/H&R Block.
